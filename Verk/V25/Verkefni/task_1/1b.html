Tangerine Dream

Tangerine Dream are a German electronic music group founded in 1967 by Edgar Froese.
The band underwent many personnel changes over the years, with Froese being the only
continuous member. Drummer and composer Klaus Schulze was briefly a member of an early
lineup. The most stable version of the group, during their influential mid-1970s period, was as a
trio with Froese, Christopher Franke, and Peter Baumann. In the late 1970s, Johannes
Schmoelling replaced Baumann, and this lineup was stable and extremely productive as well.

Although the group released numerous studio and live recordings, a substantial number of
their fans were introduced to Tangerine Dream by their film soundtracks, which total over sixty and
include Sorcerer, Thief, Risky Business, The Keep, Firestarter, Legend, Near Dark, Shy
People, and Miracle Mile. They composed the original score for the video game Grand Theft
Auto V.
